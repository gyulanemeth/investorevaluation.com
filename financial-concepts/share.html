Share ​

A share, in finance, refers to a unit of ownership in a company or corporation. Shares are typically represented by stock certificates, and shareholders are entitled to a portion of the company's profits and to vote on certain matters related to the company's operations.

When a company issues shares, it is raising capital by selling ownership stakes to investors. Shareholders may receive dividends if the company generates profits, and they may also benefit if the value of the shares increases over time, allowing them to sell their shares at a profit.

Shares can be publicly traded on stock exchanges, where buyers and sellers can trade shares in real-time. Publicly traded shares are subject to supply and demand and may fluctuate in value based on a variety of factors, such as market conditions, company performance, and investor sentiment.

Shares can be classified in different ways, such as common shares or preferred shares, and different types of shares may have different rights and privileges. For example, preferred shares may have priority over common shares when it comes to receiving dividends or in the event of a liquidation.
